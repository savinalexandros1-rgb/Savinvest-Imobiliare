# Hotel Palace Busteni

Former Casino & Spa Establishment, founded in 1907

## Core Positioning

Hotel Palace Busteni is a rare large-scale historic property in the heart of Romania's Prahova Valley, offering approximately 7,700 sqm of built area on a land plot of approximately 4,300 sqm. Originally developed in 1907 and associated with the cultural legacy of Ioan Slavici, the building later operated as a spa establishment and casino, giving it both historical narrative and strong destination appeal.

Located in Busteni, at the foot of the Bucegi Mountains, the asset combines architectural identity, central mountain resort positioning, and a scale that is increasingly difficult to source in established European tourism corridors at this entry price point.

## Long Listing Copy

Hotel Palace Busteni presents a compelling redevelopment opportunity for investors seeking a landmark-scale asset in one of Romania's best-known mountain resort destinations.

Built in 1907 and later transformed into a casino and spa establishment, the property has remained one of Busteni's most distinctive buildings for over a century. Today, it offers approximately 7,700 sqm of built area on approximately 4,300 sqm of land, with water, sewage, electricity, and natural gas already available.

The property's location is a major part of its investment case. Busteni benefits from year-round tourism demand driven by ski access, hiking routes, mountain recreation, and steady domestic leisure traffic from Bucharest. The asset is positioned approximately 135 km from Bucharest, approximately 45 km from Brasov, and approximately 55 km from Brasov-Ghimbav International Airport, with Cantacuzino Castle and the wider Sinaia-Busteni tourism corridor nearby.

From a repositioning perspective, the building is suited to several concept directions, including a boutique hotel, wellness retreat, private clinic, medical recovery concept, conference and events venue, educational use, or senior living project. Its visual identity, footprint, and internal volume give it the kind of character that can support a strong branded redevelopment story.

According to seller-provided information, the property is not currently listed as a protected historic monument, which may allow greater redevelopment flexibility than a formally listed heritage asset. Any buyer should independently verify planning, zoning, monument, and legal status during due diligence.

Asking price: EUR 1,550,000, negotiable.
