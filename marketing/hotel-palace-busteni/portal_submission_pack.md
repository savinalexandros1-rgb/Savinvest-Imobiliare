### Full Description

Hotel Palace Busteni presents a compelling redevelopment opportunity for investors seeking a landmark-scale asset in one of Romania's best-known mountain resort destinations.

Built in 1907 and later transformed into a casino and spa establishment, the property has remained one of Busteni's most distinctive buildings for over a century. Today, it offers approximately 7,700 sqm of built area on approximately 4,300 sqm of land, with water, sewage, electricity, and natural gas already available.

The location is a major part of the investment case. Busteni benefits from year-round tourism demand driven by ski access, hiking routes, mountain recreation, and steady domestic leisure traffic from Bucharest. The asset is located approximately 135 km from Bucharest, approximately 45 km from Brasov, and approximately 55 km from Brasov-Ghimbav International Airport, with Cantacuzino Castle and the wider Sinaia-Busteni tourism corridor nearby.

From a repositioning perspective, the building is suited to several concept directions, including a boutique hotel, wellness retreat, private clinic, medical recovery concept, conference and events venue, educational use, or senior living project. Its architecture, scale, and identity support a strong branded redevelopment story.

According to seller-provided information, the property is not currently listed as a protected historic monument. Buyers should verify all legal, planning, zoning, and redevelopment matters independently during due diligence.

Asking price: EUR 1,550,000, negotiable.
